Role

As Captain, Co-Founder, and Lead Powertrain Engineer for UT Longhorn Baja Racing, I led a team of 12 engineers through the full design, manufacturing, and integration of the powertrain system for the 2026 Baja SAE competition vehicle. This included setting technical direction, assigning subsystem ownership, and ensuring alignment between design, manufacturing, and validation efforts. I was responsible for driving the system from concept to a fully functional, competition-ready implementation under strict timeline and resource constraints.

Engineering

I led the development of a CVT-based powertrain system utilizing a jackshaft and chain drive architecture to efficiently transfer power from the engine to the drivetrain. The design process required balancing performance, reliability, manufacturability, and strict SAE rule compliance.

Key contributions included:

  • Designing and packaging the CVT system, jackshaft assembly, and chain drive layout within tight spatial constraints
  • Engineering protective guarding for both the CVT and chain drive to meet safety regulations and prevent pinch-point hazards
  • Performing trade studies on gear ratios, chain selection, and shaft design to optimize torque delivery and acceleration performance
  • Leading design for manufacturing decisions across all custom components, ensuring parts could be produced using available machining, welding, and fabrication capabilities
  • Managing CAD development in SolidWorks and overseeing assembly integration and tolerance stack-up considerations

I also directed the manufacturing phase, coordinating machining, fabrication, and assembly efforts to translate design intent into a reliable physical system.

Validation

I worked closely with the systems and validation teams to ensure the powertrain performed reliably under real-world competition conditions. This included both analytical and experimental validation methods.

A key effort was leading a CFD-based thermal and airflow analysis of the CVT housing to evaluate cooling performance under sustained high-load operation. This analysis helped identify airflow limitations and informed design adjustments to improve heat dissipation and prevent performance degradation during endurance events.

Additional validation work included:

  • DFMEA-driven identification and mitigation of multiple failure modes across the drivetrain
  • Root-cause analysis of test issues and iterative design improvements
  • Confirmation testing of critical components under load conditions representative of competition use

This cross-functional collaboration ensured that the system was not only functional, but robust and competition-ready.

Impact

The final powertrain system delivered measurable improvements in both performance and efficiency. Through targeted design trade-offs and weight-conscious engineering, we reduced total vehicle mass by approximately 8 lb, directly improving acceleration and responsiveness.

Beyond raw performance, the system was designed to be:

  • More reliable under sustained high-load conditions
  • Safer through integrated guarding and compliance with competition standards
  • Easier to manufacture and service, reducing build complexity for the team

The project demonstrates my ability to lead a multidisciplinary engineering team, take a system from concept to reality, and make design decisions that translate into real, track-level performance gains.
